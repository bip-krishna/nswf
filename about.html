About NSWF

Our Story & Mission

Awareness and collective action can transform society. NSWF is driven by the belief that every individual has the power to create change.

Nisarg Srishti Welfare Foundation (NSWF) is a Pune-based non-profit organization dedicated to creating a peaceful and sustainable society through awareness and community action. Established with the vision of creating a peaceful and sustainable world, the foundation works on social causes that directly impact the well-being of individuals and communities.

The flagship mission focuses on reducing noise pollution, a modern-day environmental challenge that is often overlooked but deeply affects health, productivity, and quality of life. Through interactive workshops, awareness campaigns, and research-driven solutions, NSWF helps people understand the harmful effects of noise and guides them towards practical ways of reducing it in daily life.

Beyond noise pollution, NSWF is equally committed to women empowerment, career guidance for youth, environmental conservation, and community development. The organization engages directly with people at schools, colleges, corporates, and local communities, ensuring its work brings visible, grassroots impact.

Vision

A peaceful, healthy, and environmentally responsible India where every citizen values harmony with nature and human well-being.

Mission

To work towards human welfare, women empowerment, environmental conservation, education, culture, and literature. The organization aims to create a positive impact on society by contributing to these areas through awareness, education, and community action.

Founder

Dr. Garima Kavathekar

Dr. Garima Kavathekar is the Founder & Director of Nisarg Srishti Welfare Foundation. A TEDx speaker, award-winning social worker, and advocate for sustainable living, she leads the foundation with a clear mission — to build a society where harmony with nature and respect for human well-being go hand in hand.

Her work has been recognized internationally, with NSWF's initiatives featured by BBC World Service on People Fixing the World and in a France 24 documentary on noise pollution in India.
