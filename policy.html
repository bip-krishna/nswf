Organizational policies

Our Standards & Values

NSWF is committed to ethical, transparent, and responsible operations. Our policies reflect the values we uphold in every aspect of our work.

Governance

HR & Organizational Policies

1. Code of Conduct

All employees, volunteers, board members, and representatives of NSWF are expected to uphold the highest standards of integrity, honesty, and professionalism. Conduct must reflect the mission and values of the foundation at all times. Any activity that brings the organization into disrepute, or violates legal or ethical standards, is grounds for disciplinary action.

2. Equal Opportunity Employment

NSWF is an equal opportunity employer. We celebrate diversity and are committed to creating an inclusive environment for all employees and volunteers regardless of race, caste, religion, gender, sexual orientation, age, disability, or socioeconomic background.

3. Anti-Harassment Policy

NSWF maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward harassment of any kind, including sexual harassment, bullying, discrimination, and intimidation. Any incident should be reported immediately to the designated Grievance Officer.

4. Leave & Attendance Policy

Employees are entitled to earned leave, casual leave, sick leave, and national holidays as per the foundation's leave calendar. All leave requires prior approval from the reporting manager except in emergencies.

5. Volunteer Policy

Volunteers are the backbone of NSWF's grassroots work. All volunteers must register with the foundation and agree to abide by this Code of Conduct. Volunteers under 18 require parental or guardian consent.

6. Child Protection & Safeguarding Policy

NSWF is committed to protecting the safety and well-being of all children who participate in our programs. All staff and volunteers working with children must undergo a background verification and adhere to strict safeguarding guidelines.

7. Data Protection & Privacy Policy

NSWF collects and processes personal data — including names, contact information, photographs, and participation records — solely for program and communication purposes. We do not share personal data with third parties without explicit consent.

8. Whistleblower Policy

NSWF encourages employees, volunteers, and stakeholders to report any suspected misconduct, fraud, corruption, or violation of policy without fear of retaliation.

9. Health & Safety Policy

NSWF is committed to providing a safe and healthy working environment for all personnel. All field activities must include a risk assessment and appropriate safety measures.
