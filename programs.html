dB monitoring program

Ecominds@Environment
in Detail

The Ecominds@Environment pillar focuses on collecting real-world decibel data to understand noise pollution patterns across Pune. Using calibrated sound level meters compliant with IS 9779 / IEC 61672 standards, NSWF deploys monitoring teams at strategic locations during peak traffic hours, festivals, and public events.

Equipment & Methodology

NSWF uses Class 2 Sound Level Meters (dB Trek and equivalent models) with the following specifications:

  • Measurement range: 30–130 dB(A)
  • Frequency weighting: A, C, and Z
  • Time weighting: Fast (125 ms), Slow (1 s), and Impulse
  • Data logging with real-time timestamps and GPS geotags
  • Calibration verified before and after each deployment

Monitoring Locations

Noise levels are measured at 15+ key locations across Pune Metropolitan Region, categorized into:

  • Traffic Junctions: Shivajinagar, Swargate, Hadapsar, Aundh, Katraj
  • Festival Hotspots: Ganesh Utsav mandals, Diwali market areas, procession routes
  • Silent Zones: Hospital precincts, school zones, residential colonies
  • Public Spaces: Railway stations, bus stands, market areas, religious sites

Data Applications

All collected data is archived and analyzed to:

  • Generate noise maps of Pune highlighting pollution hotspots
  • Support academic research (collaboration with GIPE for Ganesh Utsav 2025 study)
  • Inform advocacy campaigns and policy recommendations to municipal authorities
  • Educate communities with location-specific evidence during awareness sessions
  • Track year-over-year trends to measure impact of awareness initiatives
